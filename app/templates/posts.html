Kyrecce Lisbie Welcome Post

27/06/2026

Here is a look at some of Kyreece Lisbie's last season stats compared to one of his teammates, Harry Anderson, and a player similar in position and league finish, Calum Agius of Crewe Alexandra

First we look at Goals and Assists where Lisbie is close to his Harry, and is ahead of Calum by a decent amount

Next we look at the shots of each player, in which we can see Lisbie is ahead of both players in all values, showing how he could be consistent in getting shots off and getting them on target

When looking at the players passing, we can see that Lisbie is behind on all passing values, suggesting that he might be more of an attacking winger who focuses on getting down the pitch rather than building up play

Finally, Looking at some attacking stats its clear that Lisbie exceeds in getting forward with the ball, with more dribbles and a lot more touches in the opposition box, once again suggesting that Lisbie is best played as a high line attacking midfielder

Overall, I think this a brilliant signing for both the club and Leam. He should be a worthy investment who could be sold for a decent amount and also suits Leam's philosophy of fast wingers who get into the box and get shots off

Udoka Godwin-Malife

30/06/2026

Here is a look at new signing Udoka Godwin-Malife, compared with some of his new teammates, Paudie O'Connor and Jeriel Dorsett.

Looking at Udoka passing, he is close compared to his standard pass completion to Paudie and Jeriel, while his long balls are much more accurate. His crossing is down compared to the others, maybe suggesting he struggles with crossing, but he has completed more crosses then both.

When comparing the players duel percentages, we can see that his ground duels is below the others, while being between the others when it comes to aerial duels. This could suggest that he is not as confident when it comes to contesting the ball in close quarter situations.

Looking at their defensive contributions, we can see where Udoka excels, with his recoveries miles above the others, highlighting his ability to catch up to long balls and recover the ball. He does fall behind tackles and interceptions compared to the others.

Overall, despite some of the data suggesting he isn't as defensively sound as his teammates, I think he will do well playing alongside Paudie, in a back 2 or 3, with Paudie being able to win more duels and tackles, while Udoka can help with recoveries behind the defensive line.
